About Soft Drink Awareness

Our mission to expose the truth about sugary beverages

Our Story

Soft Drink Awareness was founded in 2020 by a group of health professionals, nutritionists, and concerned citizens who witnessed firsthand the devastating effects of excessive soft drink consumption on individuals and communities.

What began as a local initiative to educate school children about healthy beverage choices has grown into a national movement to raise awareness about the hidden dangers in every sip of soda and other sugary drinks.

Our Mission

To provide evidence-based information about the health impacts of soft drinks, empower individuals to make informed beverage choices, and advocate for policies that reduce the harmful effects of excessive sugar consumption on public health.

Why We Do This Work

We believe everyone has the right to accurate information about what they're putting into their bodies. The beverage industry spends billions on marketing that downplays or obscures the health risks of their products.

Our team has seen the consequences:

  • Children developing type 2 diabetes before they reach high school
  • Families struggling with obesity-related health issues
  • Communities with limited access to clean water but abundant soda availability
  • The normalization of daily soft drink consumption as harmless

We're not against occasional treats, but we are against the deception that has made soft drinks a daily staple in millions of diets worldwide.

Our Approach

Science-Based Education

All our materials are grounded in peer-reviewed research from leading medical journals and health organizations. We translate complex scientific findings into clear, actionable information.

Community Empowerment

We work with schools, community centers, and healthcare providers to deliver programs that help people understand beverage choices and develop healthier habits.

Policy Advocacy

We support evidence-based policies like sugar taxes, clearer labeling, and restrictions on marketing to children - policies that have been shown to reduce consumption and improve public health.

Positive Solutions

We don't just highlight problems - we provide practical alternatives and strategies for reducing soft drink consumption without feeling deprived.

Our Impact

Since our founding, Soft Drink Awareness has:

  • Reached over 500,000 students through school programs
  • Trained 2,000 healthcare providers to discuss beverage choices with patients
  • Supported successful policy initiatives in 12 states
  • Helped thousands of individuals reduce or eliminate sugary drinks
  • Been featured in major media outlets bringing national attention to the issue

But with soft drink consumption still at alarming levels, our work is far from done.
